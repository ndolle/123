In the context of the European Union, the "Legislative Train" refers to the formal, tracked schedule of how a policy moves from a strategic idea to a binding law across all Member States.

For the **European Quantum Act**, the legislative train is moving incredibly fast due to the technology's strategic and geopolitical importance. Here is the roadmap based on the current EU trajectory:

### 1. The Pre-Legislative Phase (Completed)

* **July 2025 (The Strategy):** The European Commission published the *Quantum Europe Strategy*. This was the "soft law" announcement that set the policy goals and officially signaled that a binding Quantum Act was coming.
* **October – December 2025 (Call for Evidence):** The EU opened a public consultation phase. They gathered impact assessments from industry leaders (like Tier-1 banks, tech firms, and infrastructure operators) to ensure the upcoming rules would be workable and proportionate.

### 2. "Tabled" Status (Approaching Q2 2026)

* **April – June 2026:** This is the most critical upcoming milestone. The European Commission will formally present the legislative proposal for the European Quantum Act.
* Once submitted to the European Parliament and the Council of the EU, the initiative's status on the legislative train officially changes to **"Tabled,"** meaning the drafting and amendment process begins.

### 3. The Ordinary Legislative Procedure (2026 – 2027)

* **Committee Reviews:** The Parliament will assign the file to specific committees (likely the Committee on Industry, Research and Energy - ITRE) to debate the text, add amendments, and draft a report. The Council of the EU (representing Member State governments) will simultaneously form its own position.
* **Trilogues:** The Parliament, Council, and Commission will enter closed-door interinstitutional negotiations to hash out a final, agreed-upon text. Expect heavy lobbying here from the financial and tech sectors regarding the specific mandates for Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) and funding access.

### 4. "Close to Adoption" & Implementation (Late 2027 – 2030)

* **Final Vote:** Once a provisional agreement is reached, it will be formally voted on and adopted.
* **Going Live:** The Act will become binding law. This will officially unlock the massive public-private funding frameworks, establish specialized oversight bodies (like the proposed EU Office of Quantum Technology Assessment), and enforce strict compliance timelines for critical infrastructure (like banks) to migrate to quantum-safe security networks.

**Why this matters to a bank:** By the time the train reaches the end of the line (Adoption), compliance is no longer optional. Banks must use the current window (2026–2027) to pilot their Post-Quantum Cryptography transitions *before* the rigid regulatory deadlines and fines are locked into EU law.

While the official draft of the **European Quantum Act** won't be formally tabled until Q2 2026, the European Commission’s preparatory documents, public consultations, and the recent *Quantum Europe Strategy* make it very clear what will be expected of companies.

The core message from regulators is that quantum is no longer just a research experiment; it is highly strategic, dual-use (civilian and military) infrastructure. Once the Act is adopted, companies—especially those in critical sectors like finance, telecommunications, and defense—will face binding obligations.

Here is what companies are expected to do under the upcoming EU Quantum Act:

### 1. Mandatory Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) Migration

For banks and critical infrastructure operators, this is the heaviest expectation. Companies will be legally expected to secure their data against the "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" threat.

* **The Expectation:** Companies must transition their digital infrastructure to quantum-resistant encryption algorithms. The Quantum Act will likely establish strict, legally binding timelines for this migration, acting as a technical extension of existing cybersecurity laws like DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act) and NIS2.

### 2. Strict "Dual-Use" Compliance and Export Controls

Because quantum computing and quantum sensors have massive military applications (breaking encryption, stealth detection), the EU treats them as a matter of national security.

* **The Expectation:** Companies developing or integrating quantum technologies will be subject to strict "dual-use" regulations. You will be expected to track exactly where your technology is going, enforce strict export controls, and prove you are preventing Intellectual Property (IP) leakage to non-allied foreign nations.

### 3. Supply Chain Audits and "Sovereignty"

A major goal of the Act is to stop Europe from relying on the US or China for next-generation technology (like they currently do with standard microchips).

* **The Expectation:** Companies will be required to conduct deep supply chain risk assessments. If you are building or buying quantum hardware, you will be expected to monitor critical inputs (like specialized cryogenics or photonic chips) and prove your supply chain is resilient and not overly dependent on foreign vendors that could cut off access during a geopolitical crisis.

### 4. Adherence to Harmonized European Standards

The EU wants to avoid a fragmented market where every country builds incompatible quantum machines.

* **The Expectation:** Companies will be expected to adopt unified European standards currently being drafted by bodies like ETSI and CEN/CENELEC. Whether it is a certification scheme for a Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) network or interoperability standards for quantum software, companies must build and buy "to spec" to legally operate across the single market.

### 5. Transitioning from "Lab to Market" (Co-Financing & Upskilling)

The EU is pouring billions of euros into shared infrastructure, like the six planned quantum chip pilot lines and the EuroQCI (quantum communication internet). In return, they expect the private sector to step up.

* **The Expectation:** Tech companies and corporate end-users (like Tier-1 banks) are expected to co-invest. You are expected to utilize these public testbeds, transition your theoretical use cases into commercialized products, and actively invest in upskilling your workforce (potentially tying into the newly announced European Quantum Skills Academy).

**In Summary:**
The EU Quantum Act expects companies to treat quantum technology not as a standard IT upgrade, but as **critical, heavily regulated national infrastructure.** If a company wants to operate in the European market, it must prove its quantum supply chains are sovereign, its encryption is quantum-safe, and its technology is secure from foreign adversaries.

### **What does EuroQCI stand for?**

EuroQCI stands for the **European Quantum Communication Infrastructure**.

### **What is EuroQCI Live?**

"EuroQCI Live" is the highly critical strategic milestone, set for **2030**, when this sovereign, continent-wide "quantum shield" is slated to become fully operational and legally enforced for high-risk data flows.

Here is exactly what this infrastructure does, simplified into three core points:

#### **1. A Sovereign, Unhackable "Quantum Shield"**

EuroQCI is a major EU initiative, designed to integrate quantum cryptography and communication networks throughout the European Union. Its goal is to create a physical communication infrastructure that is **mathematically impossible to intercept or hack**, using the fundamental laws of quantum physics.

It achieves this by generating and distributing perfectly random, unforgeable cryptographic keys via a technology called **Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)**.

#### **2. The 2030 Mandate for High-Risk Data**

By 2030 (the "EuroQCI Live" milestone), the most critical EU data flows will likely be mandated to run across this sovereign network to ensure total independence from foreign infrastructure. This includes:

* **Governmental Communications:** Securing highly sensitive classified data.
* **Central Bank Transfers:** Protecting systemic financial flows.
* **Critical Energy Grids:** Ensuring the stability of essential infrastructure.
* **Tier-1 Financial Systems:** Securing systemic financial operations.

#### **3. The Technology: Ground and Space**

EuroQCI will combine two segments to provide comprehensive, secure coverage:

* **Terrestrial Segment:** A core network of optical fiber cables connecting critical sites and capital cities across the EU.
* **Space Segment:** Leveraging initiatives like the **IRIS²** satellite program to beam quantum keys down to locations that are not easily connected by fiber.

In summary, **EuroQCI Live** is not just a technological capability; it is a legally enforced "Quantum Sovereign" mandate arriving in 2030. High-risk entities, including systemic banks, will be expected to route their most sensitive data through this sovereign shield to protect against the catastrophic security risks posed by both current "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" strategies and future quantum decryption capabilities.




The forthcoming **EU Quantum Act** (expected to be proposed in 2026) is the legislative engine designed to implement the broader **Quantum Europe Strategy**, which aims to position Europe as the global leader in quantum technology by 2030. 

The strategy and the forthcoming Act are built upon **five interconnected strategic pillars**:

### 1. Research and Innovation
The EU aims to consolidate its world-class scientific excellence to lead in quantum science and its industrial transformation. This will be driven by the launch of the **Quantum Europe Research and Innovation Initiative**, which will coordinate joint efforts between the EU and Member States to support foundational research and practical applications.

### 2. Quantum Infrastructures
This pillar focuses on developing scalable, coordinated infrastructure hubs. Key initiatives include deploying state-of-the-art quantum computers integrated with European supercomputers (EuroHPC), launching a pilot facility for the European Quantum Internet by 2026, and deploying a secure quantum communication infrastructure (EuroQCI) spanning the entire EU. 

### 3. Strengthening the Quantum Ecosystem (Industrialization)
To transition from lab experiments to commercial products, the EU plans to secure supply chains and scale up industrial capacity. Actions include establishing a quantum design facility, creating six quantum chip pilot lines to transform prototypes into manufacturable products, and eventually building an EU quantum foundry. 

### 4. Space and Dual-Use Quantum Technologies
Recognizing that quantum technology has significant military and security applications, this pillar focuses on integrating secure, sovereign quantum capabilities into Europe's space, security, and defense strategies. This includes developing a Quantum Technology Roadmap in Space with the European Space Agency and contributing to the European armament technological roadmap.

### 5. Quantum Skills
To address the severe global talent shortage, the EU is prioritizing workforce development. This includes expanding Quantum Competence Clusters and establishing the **European Quantum Skills Academy** in 2026 to build a diverse, highly skilled workforce.
